Which is right for you?

Standby vs. Portable

Both options keep your home powered during an outage. We'll help you choose the right solution for your needs and budget.

Standby Generator
Most Popular
  • Automatic transfer switch — starts itself when power goes out
  • Natural gas or propane — no refueling required
  • Whole-home coverage
  • Runs indefinitely while fuel supply lasts
  • Quiet operation
Portable + Transfer Switch
Budget-Friendly
  • Essential circuits covered
  • Manual transfer switch
  • Lower upfront cost
  • Gasoline or dual-fuel

Common Questions

Generator FAQ

Answers to the questions we hear most often.

The right size depends on what you want to power. A whole-home standby generator typically ranges from 11kW to 22kW for most Connecticut homes. During your free in-home assessment, we calculate your home's load and recommend the properly sized unit — so you're not overpaying for capacity you don't need, or undersizing and running into problems.

Yes. Generator installations in Connecticut require an electrical permit, and in most towns, a building permit as well. We handle all of this for you — from filing the paperwork to scheduling the final inspection. Every generator we install is done by the book, so you're covered.

We install all major generator brands including Generac, Kohler, Briggs & Stratton, and Cummins. We're familiar with the strengths of each and can help you choose a unit that fits your home, budget, and fuel source.

Most standby generator installations are completed in one day. The timeline depends on the complexity of the installation — panel work, gas line routing, and permit scheduling can add time. We'll give you a realistic schedule upfront and keep you informed at every step.

We can advise you on recommended maintenance schedules. Standby generators should be serviced annually — oil and filter changes, spark plug inspection, and a load test. Ask us about our maintenance options when you schedule your installation.
